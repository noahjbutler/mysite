Philosophy Paper: Plato Analysis

A lack of experience makes for a personal truth, which supersedes any seeming world truth to the individual.

To be the chained prisoner in a cave is to lack experience, but what good does experience do to an unhappy individual. It is quite possible that an unlearned man is a far of a deal happier than the learned man. Sure, maybe the meaning of existence isn’t pure happiness to everyone, but does that mean the so called “chained prisoners” are truly chained? If meaning is fulfilled, and the so called chained prisoners are content with their life and the things they know, the chains are broken.
